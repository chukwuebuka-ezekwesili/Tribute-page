Abraham Lincoln (1809-1865)

An aberrant man who became the Sixteenth President of America, waged a political contest and civil war that protected the union, ended slavery and gave African-Americans a chance at civil and social freedom.

Here`s a detailed timeline of Abraham Lincoln`s life:

1809-1823:

  • 1809, 12th Feb - Born in Hardin County (now LaRue County), Kentucky, to Thomas Lincoln(1778-1851) and Nancy Hanks Lincoln (1784-1818).
  • 1811, Spring - Moved with family to a farm in Knob Creek, Kentucky.
  • 1815-1816 - Attended occassional classes in rural schoolhouse.
  • 1816, Fall - Moved with Family to Indiana.
  • 1818, 5th Oct - Mother Nancy Hanks Lincoln died of milk sickness, Spencer County, Indiana. Thomas Lincoln subsequently left Abraham Lincoln and older sister Sarah Lincoln (1807-1828) alone in Indiana while he traveled to Kentucky to remarry.
  • 1819, 9th Dec - Thomas Lincoln married Sarah Bush Johnston (1788-1869) in Elizabethtown, Kentucky. Sarah Johnston Lincoln had three children of her own (Elizabeth, Matilda and John), but developed a close bond with Abraham Lincoln.
  • 1819-1824 - Additional schooling when available.
  • 1819-1830 - Laborer on family farm and for neighbors.

1824-1837

  • 1828, 20th Jan - Sister Sarah Lincoln Grigsby died in childbirth.
  • 1828 - Ferryman on the Ohio River; helped take a flatboat cargo from Indiana to New Orleans, Louisiana.
  • 1830, Mar - Moved with family to Illinois, settling in Macon County.
  • 1831, Apr-July - Helped build a flatboat and ferried cargo from Sangamon County, Illinois, to New Orleans, Louisiana.
  • 1831 - Clerk, general store, New Salem, Illinois.
  • 1832, 7th Apr - Elected Captain, Thirty-first Regiment, Illinois Militia, in the Black Hawk War.
  • 1832, 6th Aug - Defeated in election for a seat in the Illinois General Assembly.
  • 1832-1836 - Shopkeeper, surveyor, and postmaster in New Salem, Illinois.
  • 1834 - Began to read Law.
  • 1834, 4th Aug - Elected as Whig candidate from Sangamon County to the lower house of the Illinois General Assembly, which then met in Vandalia, Illinois.
  • 1835, 25th Aug - Ann Ruthledge, Lincoln`s New Salem friend, died. Some Lincoln associates claimed after Lincoln`s death that she was his first great love.
  • 1836, 6th Sept - Licensed to practice law.
  • 1836-1842 - Reelected for three additional terms, Sangamon County representative, Illinois General Assembly. He was one of the so called "Long Nine" in the Sangamon delegation notable for above-average height and being instrumental role in moving the statecapital to Springfield.
  • 1837, 15th Apr - Moved to Springfield, Illinois, to become law partner with John T. Stuart in the firm of Stuart and Lincoln.
  • 1837, 16th Aug - Wrote to prospective fiancee Mary S. Owens of willingness to continue their relationship. Owens did not reply and the association ended.

1838-1851

  • 1838, 27th Jan - Delivered address on "The Perpetuation of Our Political Institution" to Young Men`s Lyceum of Springfield.
  • 1839 - Mary Todd began living in Springfield, the new capital of Illinois, with her sister Elizabeth Todd Edwards, the wife of Ninian W. Edwards, the son of former Illinois governor Ninian Edwards and an acquaintance of Abraham Lincoln.
  • 1841, 1st Jan - Ended initial engagement with Mary Todd; suffered period of depression. Romance with Mary Todd resumed in 1842.
  • 1841, Apr

    Partnership of Stuart $ Lincoln dissolved

    - Partnership established with Stephen T. Logan in the law firm of Logan & Lincoln, Springfield, Illinois; dissolved in 1844
  • 1842, 4th Nov - Married to Mary Todd (1818-1882), daughter of Robert Smith Todd (1791-1849) and Elizabeth Parker Todd (1794-1825) of Lexington, Kentucky.
  • 1843, 1st Aug - Son Robert Todd Lincoln (D. 27th July, 1926) was born
  • 1844, Jan - Purchased house on the corner of Eighth and Jackson streets in Springfield, Illinois.
  • 1844, Nov - Formed law partnership with Williams H. Herndon in the firm of Lincoln & Herndon, Springfield, Illinois.
  • 1846, 10th Mar - Son Edward Baker "Eddie" Lincoln (D. 1st Feb, 1850) born.
  • 1846, 3rd Aug - Elected from the Whig Party to the United States House of Representatives; assumed office in December 1847.
  • 1847, July - Attended Chicago River and Harbor Convention in July.
  • 1847, Nov - Visited Mary Lincoln`s family in Lexington, Kentucky, en route to Washington, D.C.
  • 1847-1849 - Served single term representing Illinois in the U.S.House of the Representatives, December 6, 1847-March 4, 1849. Lived in Mrs. Sprigg`s Carroll Row boarding house on Capitol Hill.
  • 1847, Oct - Argued Matson Slave Case in which he represented a slave owner seeking to reclaim slaves used as seasonal labor in Illinois, where slavery was outlawed.
  • 1847, 22nd Dec - Introduced "Spot Resolutions" in House of Representatives, challenging President James K. Polk to prove that the "spot" of land on which American blood had been shed (the event that prompted the United States to declare war on Mexico) had infact been shed on American soil.
  • 1848, June - Attended Whig Party`s national convention in Philadelphia.
  • 1848, Sept - Traveled through New England on a speaking tour.
  • 1849, 7th Mar Admitted to the bar of the United States Supreme Court.
  • 1849, 22nd May - Granted patent No.6469 for floatation devices to lift boats over shoals; had applied for patent on March 10, 1849.
  • 1849, July - Resumed law practice, Springfield, Illinois.
  • 1849, Sept - Declined appointment to be governor of the Oregon Territory.
  • 1850, 21st Dec - Son William Wallace "Willie" Lincoln (D. 15th July, 1871) born.

1852-1865

  • 1853, 4th April - Son Thomas "Tad" Lincoln (D. 15th July, 1871) born.
  • 1854, May - Passage of Kansas-Nebraska Act allowing slavery to expand beyond the boundaries set by Missouri Compromise of 1820 inspired Lincoln to return to politics.
  • 1854, 16th Oct - Delivered three-hour speech in Peoria, Illinois, while campaigning for a seat in the Illinois House of Representatives.
  • 1855, 8th Feb - Unsuccessful Whig candidate in Illinois for United States Senate in February.
  • 1855, June - Retained for the defense in McCormick V. Manny Co patent infringement lawsuit. Lincoln worked on the "The Reaper Case" over the summer and attended the trail in Cincinnati in September, but he was excluded by other members of the defense team, including his future Secretary of War Edwin McMasters Stanton
  • 1856, 19th June - Recieved 110 votes for the vice president at the Republican National Convention in Philadelphia; Williams L. Dayton of New Jersey ultimately nominated as vice president candidate.
  • 1857, July-Sept - Represented defense in Hurd et al. v. The Railway Bridge Company case, which pit the owner of the "Effie Afton" against the owners of a bridge spanning the Mississippi River.
  • 1858, 16th June - Delivered "House Divided" speech at the Illinois Republican Convention.
  • 1858, 2nd Nov - Unsuccessful Republican candidate in the Illinois for the United States Senate.
  • 1860, 18th May - Nominated as Republican Party`s candidate for president.
  • 1860, 6th Nov - Elected president of the United states.
  • 1861, 4th Mar - Inaugurated Sixteenth president.
  • 1861, 12th Apr - Confederates in the Charleston fired on the Union-held Fort Sumter in Charleston Harbour, effectively starting the Civil War.
  • 1861, 15th Apr - Issued proclamation calling for 75,000 volunteers to quell rebellion.
  • 1861, Dec - Union forces defeated in the first major land battle of the Civil War fought at Manassas, Virginia.
  • 1861, Dec - Administration successfully resolved diplomatic dispute with Great Britian over the Trent Affair in which Confederate envoys were removed from a British vessel.
  • 1862, 20th Feb - "Willie" Lincoln died in the White House, likely of typhoid. Remains temporarily interred in the Caroll vault at the Oak Hill Cemetery in Georgetown. Mary Lincoln inconsolable.
  • 1862, 22nd Sept - Issued Preliminary Emancipation Proclamation after Union victory at Battle of Antietam on September 17th.
  • 1863, 1st Jan - Issued Final Emancipation Proclamation.
  • 1863, 8th Dec - Issued Proclamation of Amnesty and Reconstruction, which established lenient terms for former Confederates wishing to rejoin the union.
  • 1864, 8th June - Nominated as president candidate of the National Union Party (Republican Party) at convention in Baltimore; Democrate Andrew Johnson of Tennesse nominated as vice president.
  • 1864, 23rd Aug - Doubtful of his chances for re-election, wrote memorandum pledging to work with the president-elect to save the union before March 4; requested cabinet members to sign the sealed document without seeing the contents.
  • 1864, 8th Nov - Reelected president on November 8.
  • 1865, 31st Jan - U.S. House of Representatives passed joint resolution to U.S. Construction to abolish slavery. The Senate had previously passed the resolution on April 8, 1864. Lincoln signed the official resolution, and several ceremonial copies. The amendment was then sent to the states for ratification, and became the Thirteeth Amendment in December.
  • 1865, 4th Mar - Second Inaugurated.
  • 1865, 9th Apr - Confederate General Robert E. Lee surrendered the Army of Northern Virginia to General Grant at Appomattox Court House. Other Confederate troops remained in the field, but the war was effectively over.
  • 1865, 11th Apr - Delivered final public speech.
  • 1865, 14th Apr - Shot while attending a performance of "Our American Cousin" at Ford`s Theatre. Removed to the Peterson boarding house across the street from the theater.
  • 1865, 15th Apr - Died, 7:22am.
  • 1865, 19th-20th Apr - Funeral held at the White House on April 19th, and remains lay in state at the U.S. Capitol on April 19th-20th.
